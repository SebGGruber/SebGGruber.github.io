About Me

I am a computer scientist (PhD) and statistician (MSc) with a strong interest in using theoretically grounded approaches to understand the uncertainty of machine learning predictions. My current research focuses on the calibration of predicted probabilities and a probabilistic perspective of generative modelling (such as large language models). My current affiliation is the PSI division in the ESAT department of KU Leuven under the supervision of Prof. Matthew Blaschko.

Research Interests

  • Uncertainty calibration
  • Proper scoring rules (a.k.a. proper scores or proper losses) and Bregman divergences
  • Uncertainty quantification of generative models (such as diffusion models or LLMs)
  • Kernel methods
  • Applications, where reliable uncertainties are key
